Backward Moscow

by A Story Generator



Joshua Raymond had always loved backward Moscow with its scattered, squashed swamps. It was a place where he felt healthy.

He was a splendid, greedy, port drinker with fluffy fingers and wobbly abs. His friends saw him as a pong, purring patient. Once, he had even jumped into a river and saved a smoggy baby flamingo. That's the sort of man he was.

Joshua walked over to the window and reflected on his wild surroundings. The rain hammered like boating koalas.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Pete Trescothik. Pete was a tight-fisted bear with feathery fingers and spiky abs.

Joshua gulped. He was not prepared for Pete.

As Joshua stepped outside and Pete came closer, he could see the jealous glint in his eye.

"I am here because I want some more Twitter followers," Pete bellowed, in a remarkable tone. He slammed his fist against Joshua's chest, with the force of 9876 lizards. "I frigging love you, Joshua Raymond."

Joshua looked back, even more sleepy and still fingering the giant knife. "Pete, hands up or I'll shoot," he replied.

They looked at each other with ecstatic feelings, like two troubled, tall tortoises rampaging at a very helpful funeral, which had drum and bass music playing in the background and two cold-blooded uncles smiling to the beat.

Joshua regarded Pete's feathery fingers and spiky abs. He held out his hand. "Let's not fight," he whispered, gently.

"Hmph," pondered Pete.

"Please?" begged Joshua with puppy dog eyes.

Pete looked fuzzy, his body blushing like a troubled, talented teapot.

Then Pete came inside for a nice glass of port.
